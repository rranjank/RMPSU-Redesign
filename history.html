About University / History

Our History

Tracing the journey from a freedom fighter's vision of education to a growing centre of higher learning in Aligarh.

THE BEGINNING

A University Born to Honour a Legacy

Raja Mahendra Pratap Singh University, Aligarh was established by the Government of Uttar Pradesh in 2021 as a residential-cum-affiliating state university. Prime Minister Narendra Modi laid the foundation stone of the university on 13 September 2021, dedicating the institution to the memory of Raja Mahendra Pratap Singh — a freedom fighter, educationist and social reformer whose life's work was rooted in nation-building through education.

Spread across more than 92 acres in the villages of Lodha and Musepur Kareem Jarouli in Aligarh's Kol tehsil, the university was envisioned to widen access to quality higher education across the region. It presently extends affiliation to colleges throughout the Aligarh division, carrying forward the same spirit of inclusive and accessible learning that defined the man it is named after.

Carrying the Legacy Forward

Every classroom, laboratory and library at Raja Mahendra Pratap Singh University stands on the same ideals its founder lived by — self-reliant education, social reform and service to the nation. As the university grows, it remains committed to honouring that legacy through accessible, inclusive and future-ready education.
